# District concept art — the visual north-star (2026-06-13)

Operator-supplied concept render (irwin, 2026-06-13). This is the look CityLife is steering toward:
the whole city as a glowing island that sits **on a phone/tablet** at sunset over the ocean — the
literal "the city runs inside Joe's Mac Mini" framing (see [[project_citylife]]). Pin it as the
reference for the builder visuals and the commercial vibrancy work.

## What the render shows

- **Joe the Crab at the centre** — headphones + a lightning bolt on the earcup: he is the inference /
  AI mascot, the city's host. Everything radiates out from his plaza.
- **Glowing blue neon roads** loop between every district, with **padlock pucks** sitting on the road
  segments — the gated auth boundaries (login → border → per-app allowlist). The roads ARE the
  migration spine, drawn as light.
- The districts, each a real kooker surface:
  - **Builder / construction** (top-left) — tower crane, scaffolding, blueprints, hard-hat bots, a
    wrench sign. This is **Viw the Builder's** yard (077 / 083).
  - **Cloud compute** (top-centre) — a cloud glyph over crystal-spire servers in cool blue. This is
    **kooker-service-ai** — the inference choke point rendered as a district.
  - **Analytics / commercial strip** (top-right) — a radar dish, **glowing potion-vials of colour**,
    animated **bar-chart signage**, and a **café full of bots** at the bar. THIS is the vibrancy
    target for commercial (079): neon, colour, motion, life.
  - **Sports** (bottom-left) — a pitch, a soccer ball, a trophy, a gym. **Sportifine.**
  - **Residential** (bottom-right) — cosy gabled houses with **crest signs** (a Viw wrench, an
    Antigravity wings) — every agent gets a house; the crest is its bot identity.

## The palette this implies (extends docs/specs/VISUAL-STANDARD.md)

CityLife's renderer already carries the toolkit — emissive caps, beacons, night-lit windows. The
concept just pushes the **commercial** arc far brighter than the calm residential teal:

- **Residential (today):** calm teal `0x57d1c4`, low glow — keep it homely.
- **Commercial (target):** a neon night-market. Saturated signage in magenta / cyan / amber /
  lime with **high emissive** (the vials + bar-charts), warm café interior light, and movement
  (a slow sign pulse, a bar-chart bob — `anim` is a Visual-Standard bonus, used here on purpose).
  The point of contrast: residential is where you *live*, commercial is where the city is *awake*.
- **Cloud / civic:** cool crystalline blues with the existing beacon language.
- **Roads:** the avenue already has a cool emissive; the spine reads as light at dusk — lean into it.

## How this maps to the build (visual-first, on the 079 plan)

079 (commercial plots + shops) is the arc that delivers this, but the operator wants the **vibrancy
visible early**, not gated behind the full shop economy. So sequence it visual-first:

1. **Light the district before the shops exist.** Give the reserved east commercial arc a vibrant
   treatment NOW — emissive market-stall / signage massing along the frontage (composite forms per
   the Visual Standard, deterministic, on-land per the water/slope rule) so the commercial area
   *reads* as the night-market strip even before a single shop is bought. A 079-P0 visual teaser.
2. Then the 079 mechanic underneath it (survey → buy plot → build shop massing → listing →
   storefront → real ledger checkout), each shop inheriting the vibrant signage language.
3. **Builder visuals** (the other operator ask): carry the crane/blueprint/hard-hat-bot language from
   the top-left of the render into builder.html and Viw's yard.

The money rails are already real (085-P1 — the in-game ₭ moves mirror to kooker-service-ledger), so a
vibrant commercial strip plugs straight into a live economy.
